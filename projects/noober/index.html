Noober 3D-Printing Hub

The last year, Erik and I have been working on 3d-printers jointly, and started a 3D-printing hub that offers printing services to primarily local Vancouver costumers.

The project has put us in contact with a lot of interesting people and given insight into customer service.

Wikus - custom C-Bot

As we started university, Erik decided to build a custom printer based on the C-Bot architecture. He assembled the printer for the first time in 2015. This fall I have been involved in re-assembling the printer with some updates. It has been a fun and educational build and I have learned much about the planning and execution of the assembly of such a complex unit with great requirements for precision.

Wikus is to date our most accurate printer, producing some positively astonishing prints for a FDM printer.

Robo 3D R1

We started out with the Robo and it is with us still as a 200 micron workhorse. Multiple upgrades and modifications have been required to keep the machine going, improving usability and print quality. Following in the rep-rap philosophy, Robo has made a significant part of the plastic joints and connectors for Wikus.

Octoprint

All printers in the have OctoPrint enabled, so as to allow for wireless control and monitoring. Using ZeroTire, we are able to control and monitor the printers over the internet.
