Camera quad

In 2013 I stated the making of a 500mm, foldable areal camera-platform. My challenge to myself was to manufacture the frame exclusively by the use of the 3D-printer so that the system would have to be modelled digitally.

The cam-quad has in the years since gone through multiple design iterations, evolving as I increased my knowledge of CAD-design, statics, 3D-printing and design principles.

First prototype

The first prototype frame was made exclusively from Tulman Bridge nylon filament. This material is very strong, but is more flexible than PLA/ABS. Each arm was printed separately with a compressive, horizontal member on top and a circular tensile member diagonally attached to the base of the main frame. The nylon was a temperamental material to print such large pieces with.

Vibrations

The nylon material proved too soft, amplifying the vibrations from the motor. A new iteration lead to a PLA body with nylon arms made with a different geometrical approach. This reduced the vibrations a lot and made the quad a good flyer, but the vibrations were still to large to get good footage with an onboard camera.

The project is currently halted. When I find the time to take it up again, I will abandon the concept of a solely 3D-printed quad-copter of this size.
